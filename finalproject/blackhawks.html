Will This Season be a Wake-up Call?
The Chicago Blackhawks had by far the worst season in the last 10 seasons and its not even close.
After multiple seasons of being bouunced in the first rounds of the playoffs the blackhawks
decided that they needed to make the team younger and cheaper and with that their was the
possibility of a bad season or two in route to being a younger and more sustainable
team after being strapped for cap space and not reaching the goals of the team. Which
would be winning it all.



Moving Forward
The future is bright the Blackhawks had a rookie contribute for the first time in almost the entire
Joel Quennevillie era he is an old school guy who is not know for giving rookies any signifigant playing
time. On top of that they still have one of the most prolific scorers of our time in Patrick Kane, who
was just named to be the captain of team USA this summer. We also have a great on and off ice leader
in Jonathon Toews, who also happens to still be one of the top centers on ice. Not to mention
Duncan Keith who is one of the best defenders in the league. But the rel problem is can we get
rid of all the over priced mid tier players and somehow dump the huge cap hits of the aging players
that no longer can compete at a high level. Moving forward it will be great to see what magic Stan
Bowman can creat because he certainly has done a great job in the past, so even after a terrible season
I still have great faith in our organization.
